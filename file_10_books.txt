## Title: 1984
A dystopian story about a totalitarian society controlled by surveillance, propaganda, and thought police. Winston Smith, the protagonist, secretly rebels against the system in search of truth and freedom.  
Themes: totalitarianism, freedom, resistance, manipulation.

## Title: The Hobbit
Bilbo Baggins embarks on an unexpected journey with a group of dwarves to reclaim a lost treasure guarded by the dragon Smaug. Along the way, he discovers bravery and friendship in the face of danger.  
Themes: adventure, personal growth, courage, friendship.

## Title: To Kill a Mockingbird
In the racially segregated American South, young Scout Finch learns about justice and empathy as her father defends a black man wrongly accused of a crime.  
Themes: justice, racism, innocence, moral growth.

## Title: Fahrenheit 451
In a world where books are banned, fireman Guy Montag begins to question his role in suppressing knowledge and seeks truth through forbidden literature.  
Themes: censorship, knowledge, conformity, rebellion.

## Title: The Catcher in the Rye
Holden Caulfield wanders through New York City, struggling with alienation and the transition to adulthood after being expelled from school.  
Themes: identity, isolation, adolescence, authenticity.

## Title: Brave New World
Set in a futuristic society obsessed with pleasure and control, individuals are conditioned from birth to accept their roles. Bernard Marx begins to question this utopia.  
Themes: control, individuality, technology, dehumanization.

## Title: The Great Gatsby
Jay Gatsby's obsessive love for Daisy Buchanan drives him to recreate the past in the roaring 1920s, but reality and illusion collide.  
Themes: love, illusion, wealth, the American Dream.

## Title: Animal Farm
Farm animals overthrow their human owner to create a society of equals, but corruption and power soon replicate the tyranny they fought against.  
Themes: power, corruption, equality, revolution.

## Title: Lord of the Flies
A group of boys stranded on an island try to govern themselves but descend into savagery and chaos, revealing the fragility of civilization.  
Themes: civilization vs. savagery, leadership, human nature.

## Title: Pride and Prejudice
Elizabeth Bennet navigates love, class, and family expectations while challenging societal norms and overcoming misunderstandings with Mr. Darcy.  
Themes: love, class, pride, personal growth.
